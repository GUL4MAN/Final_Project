Lechon paksiw

Paksiw na lechon is leftover spit-roasted pork (lechon) meat that is cooked with vinegar, garlic, onions, black pepper, and some water. The Luzon version adds ground liver or liver spread ("lechon sauce"), while the Visayan versions do not.



    Ingredients


  • 4 lbs. leftover Lechon or Lechon kawali
  • 3 cups Lechon sauce here is the link to our homemade authentic Lechon sauce recipe
  • 2 cups beef stock
  • 6 cloves garlic crushed
  • 2 large onions chopped
  • 1 teaspoon whole peppercorn
  • 8 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 3/4 cups vinegar
  • 3/4 cups sugar
  • salt to taste

    • Steps


  • Step 1. Heat a cooking pot and pour-in the beef stock. Bring to a boil.

  • Step 2. Put-in the garlic and onions then cook until the texture becomes soft.

  • Step 3. Add the whole peppercorns, dried bay leaves, and soy sauce.

  • Step 4. Put-in the leftover Lechon and simmer for 30 to 35 minutes.

  • Step 5. Add the vinegar and bring to a boil. Simmer for 10 minutes.

  • Step 6. Add the sugar and Lechon sauce then simmer for another 5 minutes.

  • Step 7. Dash-in some salt to taste then stir.

  • Step 8. Turn off the heat and transfer to a serving plate.

  • Step 9. Serve hot with rice. Share and enjoy!
