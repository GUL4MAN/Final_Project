Dinuguan

Dinuguan is a Filipino savory stew usually of pork offal (typically lungs, kidneys, intestines, ears, heart and snout) and/or meat simmered in a rich, spicy dark gravy of pig blood, garlic, chili (most often siling haba), and vinegar.



    Ingredients


  • 1 lb pork cut into cubes
  • 1 cup vinegar
  • 2 pieces long green pepper
  • 1 1/2 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 1 piece onion chopped finely
  • 1 tablespoon garlic minced
  • 3 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 cup water
  • 10 oz pork blood
  • 1 piece pork cube boullion
  • salt and pepper to taste

    • Steps


  • Step 1. Heat oil in a pan. Sauté garlic and onion.

  • Step 2. Once the onion softens, add pork. Continue to sauté for 5 minutes or until light brown.

  • Step 3. Pour water. Let boil.

  • Step 4. Add pork cube. Cook in low heat until the meat tenderizes.

  • Step 5. Add pork blood. Stir. Cook for 8 to 10 minutes while stirring.

  • Step 6. Pour vinegar and add sugar and long green pepper. Cook for 12 to 15 minutes.

  • Step 7. Season with salt and pepper as needed. Serve hot with puto.
