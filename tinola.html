Tinola

Tinola is a Filipino soup usually served as a main course with white rice. Traditionally, this dish is cooked with chicken or fish, wedges of papaya and/or chayote, and leaves of the siling labuyo chili pepper in broth flavored with ginger, onions and fish sauce.



    Ingredients


  • 1 whole chicken cut into serving pieces
  • 36 ounces rice washing
  • 1/2 piece green papaya cut into wedges
  • 1 tablespoon garlic minced
  • 1 piece onion chopped
  • 1 thumb ginger cut into strips
  • 2 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 cup Hot pepper leaves
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

    • Steps


  • Step 1. Sauté the garlic, onion, and ginger

  • Step 2. Put-in the chicken and cook until color turns light brown

  • Step 3. Add the fish sauce. Stir. Pour rice washing into the cooking pot. Let boil. Cover the pot and simmer for 45 minutes. Note: add water if needed.

  • Step 4. Add green papaya. Cook for 5 minutes

  • Step 5. Add the hot pepper leaves or malunggay leaves. Stir and cook for 1 minute.

  • Step 6. Season with ground black pepper. Note you can also add fish sauce or salt if needed.

  • Step 7. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve hot. Share and enjoy!
